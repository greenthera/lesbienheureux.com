Goddess of the Moon

In Inca mythology, MAMA QUILLA, deity of the Moon, is the object of an absolute cult. The Incas devote themselves body and soul to defend her. An eclipse is the sign that a snake, a lion and a puma are attacking the goddess. To help her, the Incas shoot arrows into the sky in order to frighten the beasts.

Under their protection, MAMA QUILLA ensures the regulation of the weather and ideal natural conditions, guaranteeing abundant harvests. Today, we pay tribute to this union by creating a rum with subtle, spicy and balanced aromatic profiles. Balance, elegance, smoothness, roundness and aromatic complexity.

A blend of rums from Guatemala (Solera method) with a final ageing in old Cognac casks. Modern, strongly imaginative, and evocative packaging. This rum regularly takes first place in blind tastings compared to its direct competitors.

Tasting Notes
Nose: Exotic fruit notes mingle with cinnamon and cocoa.
Palate: Smooth and honeyed.
Finish: Aromas of crème brûlée and sweet spices.
